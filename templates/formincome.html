HRA EXEMPTIONS


House Rent Allowance is given by your employer as an allowance for your rented accommodation. You can claim this HRA exemption even if your employer does not pay you any HRA or if you are self-employed. But the catch is that you should be living in rented accommodation to exempt HRA from tax deductions. People who have a place of their own and get HRA from their employer cannot reap the benefits of claiming tax deductions under the HRA exemption section. for more information you can read this acticle form clear tax. click here

Section 80DDB


Section 80DDB speaks of deductions in respect of expenses incurred for medical treatment.This Section provides that if an individual or an HUF has incurred medical expenses for treatment of specified disease or ailment, such expense is allowed as deduction, subject to such conditions and capped at such amount as specified, under Section 80DDB of Income Tax Act. Here, this speaks of medical expenses incurred on treatment of diseases or ailments and should not be confused with premium paid for health insurance bought covering such diseases or ailments.for more information, please click here

PROFESSIONAL TASK


Professional tax is a tax that is levied by a state government on all individuals who earn a living through any medium. Each state has a slab that it declares and the professional tax is deducted based on these slabs. Some states and union territories do not charge professional tax too. How it is paid is by dividing the annual professional tax due into 12 equal installments that are paid every month, except the one paid in February which is higher than the other months. if you want to calculate your Professional tax, please click here

Special Allowance


The special allowance is offered to the employees to enable them in meeting their expenses. They incur these expenses while performing their duties of an office or employment of profit. The amount varies from one company to another as per the need. It is a part of the fixed pay. You can know the details of the allowance either in the offer letter or monthly pay slip. Since it forms a part of the salary it is taxable in the hands of the employee. Special allowances are exempt from tax under section 10(14) of the Income Tax Act, 1961.For more information, please click here
